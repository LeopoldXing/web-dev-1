What are strategies ?

We use strategies all the time. We have a strategy for getting to class or work on time (set an alarm, take an early bus, put gas in the car the night before), strategies for making it through the level in a game (approach from the left, use this tool, use a cheat code), a strategy for getting in and out of the grocery store as fast as possible (make a list, organize the list by sections of the store).

So what are these "communication strategies" in the name of this course? They're a set of 11 strategies or skills you can develop to help you become a better communicator and help you build your human skills.

Psychological Safety

A group that has built psychological safety is a group that allows its members to express thoughts and ideas openly, make errors, give feedback to one another, and accept feedback from others openly and without judgement.

That's a pretty tall order!

Building psychological safety takes time and conscious effort on the part of every member of the team, but it is a key component to creating a productive team.

Psychological safety is a core concept in this course and you'll see it come up over and over again. As you saw in the Teaching and Learning Philosophy, being able to make mistakes and receive, reflect on, and incorporate feedback are really important parts of learning and leadership. In our course, as a group, we want to build psychological safety.
